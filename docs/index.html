What are Behavioural Biases?

Behavioural biases try to explain our tendency to reason in certain ways that can lead to systematic errors or deviations from a standard of rationality or good judgment in thinking or reasoning. In the financial setting, consequently, they aim to explain why consumers and investors make decisions that oppose the standard model of rational choice, which states that when presented with a set of options, individuals choose the option that maximizes their expected utility.

This site serves as an anthology of 44 biases across three major categories, in line with the taxonomy proposed by David Peon and Manel Antelo, including some examples from daily life for each.
